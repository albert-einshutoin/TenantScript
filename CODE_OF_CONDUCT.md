# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in TenantScript a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.
We act in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Positive behavior includes demonstrating empathy, respecting differing viewpoints, giving and
accepting constructive feedback, taking responsibility for mistakes, and focusing on what is best
for the community.

Unacceptable behavior includes sexualized language or attention, trolling, insulting or
derogatory comments, personal or political attacks, public or private harassment, publishing
another person's private information without permission, and conduct that would reasonably be
considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Maintainers must clarify and enforce these standards fairly and consistently. They may remove,
edit, or reject contributions that do not align with this Code, and may apply a temporary or
permanent ban for threatening, offensive, or harmful behavior. When safe, maintainers will
communicate the reason for moderation.

## Scope

This Code applies in all TenantScript project spaces and when a person officially represents the
project, including repository activity, reviews, project-managed events, and official accounts.

## Reporting and privacy

Report conduct concerns privately through
[GitHub Private Vulnerability Reporting](https://github.com/albert-einshutoin/TenantScript/security/advisories/new)
and begin the summary with `[Conduct]`. This is the repository's currently monitored confidential
maintainer channel. Do not open a public issue or include unrelated credentials, customer/tenant
data, or vulnerability details in a conduct report.

Maintainers will protect the reporter's identity and report details as confidential information
except where disclosure is required to investigate, protect a participant, or comply with law. A
maintainer named in a report must recuse themselves. Retaliation is prohibited.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers consider context, impact, history, and safety when choosing a proportionate response:

1. **Correction** — a private explanation and request to correct inappropriate behavior.
2. **Warning** — a formal warning with clear consequences for continued behavior.
3. **Temporary ban** — time-limited suspension from project interaction.
4. **Permanent ban** — permanent removal for sustained violations, harassment, aggression, or
   behavior that puts participants at risk.
